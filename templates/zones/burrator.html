    Bouldering in Burrator

    Tucked beside the scenic Burrator Reservoir on Dartmoor, Burrator Bouldering area is a granite playground offering one of the most accessible and varied bouldering experiences in the region. The standout feature of this area is its contrast of climbing styles—from steep overhangs and rare granite roof problems to delicate slab and face climbs. The rock here is noticeably less weathered than other Dartmoor granite, providing a more textured and friction-rich surface. The Lakeside Boulders sector, located mere steps from the car, and the sheltered East Side Boulders offer both convenience and protection from the elements, making this a great all-weather venue.

    Thanks to its multiple aspects and tree cover in some zones, Burrator allows for year-round bouldering, with winter often being the prime season due to cooler temperatures and reduced vegetation. The grades span from beginner-friendly to challenging testpieces, attracting both progression-focused climbers and seasoned crushers. Notable lines like "Jungle VIP" (7C+/8A) and "King of the Swingers" (7B+) have helped cement Burrator’s status as a must-visit destination on Dartmoor. With easy access, diverse problems, and a growing reputation among the local scene, Burrator continues to thrive as a core spot for granite bouldering in the southwest of England.
