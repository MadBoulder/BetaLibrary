    Bouldering in Wagon Wheel

    Wagon Wheel is a sprawling granite playground hidden in the Mojave Desert east of Ridgecrest, where the scale of the landscape defines the experience as much as the climbing itself. Instead of a compact crag, you’ll find hundreds of grey granite boulders scattered across open desert terrain, offering endless exploration and a sense of discovery that few areas can match. The climbing is classic granite style—technical footwork on edges and crystals, friction-dependent slabs, and the occasional powerful line on slopers. With over a thousand problems already established and countless more possibilities untouched, Wagon Wheel feels less like a finished destination and more like an evolving bouldering frontier.

    The best time to climb here is during the cooler months and shoulder seasons, when desert temperatures are manageable and friction is at its peak. Summer heat can be unforgiving due to the complete exposure and lack of shade. What truly sets Wagon Wheel apart is its raw, exploratory nature—there are no crowded circuits or polished classics dominating the scene, just vast terrain inviting you to wander and find your own lines. Weekdays offer the most peaceful experience, as weekends can bring off-road vehicle traffic. If you’re looking for solitude, adventure, and pure granite climbing in a remote setting, Wagon Wheel delivers in a way few areas in California can.
