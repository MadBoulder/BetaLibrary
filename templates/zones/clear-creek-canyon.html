    Bouldering in Clear Creek Canyon

    Clear Creek Canyon is one of Colorado’s most accessible and diverse bouldering destinations, offering a mix of technical and powerful climbing on schist and gneiss rock. With its steep walls, vertical faces, and polished slopers, the area caters to climbers of all levels, from V2 to V15. Some of the most iconic spots include Mile Marker 268, known for its water-polished classics like Mavericks, and Army of the Dead, where highball lines test commitment and skill. The Cat Slab and Bike Path Boulders provide a variety of problems, while the Boneyard offers a concentrated selection of challenges.

    The best season for bouldering in Clear Creek Canyon is fall through spring, as the summer heat can make conditions less ideal. However, shaded areas and early morning sessions allow year-round climbing. The landings can be uneven, making crash pads and spotters essential for safety. Seasonal raptor closures from February to July may affect certain zones, so checking access updates is crucial. Just 20 minutes from Denver, Clear Creek is a must-visit for anyone looking for quality bouldering close to the city.
