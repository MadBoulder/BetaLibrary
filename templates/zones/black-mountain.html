    Bouldering in Black Mountain

    Black Mountain, located in the San Jacinto Mountains of San Diego County, California, is renowned for its high-quality granite boulders and a wide array of challenging climbing problems. This bouldering destination offers over 1,700 problems that range from V0 to V13, providing a wide variety of challenges such as technical slabs and powerful overhangs. Its rugged terrain and diverse climbing features make it a favorite among both beginners and seasoned climbers seeking a rigorous and rewarding experience.

    The best seasons to climb at Black Mountain are spring, summer, and fall, with winter also being a viable option due to the region's mild climate. The area's high elevation and shaded forest setting offer cooler conditions, making year-round climbing feasible and enjoyable. The proximity to major cities like Los Angeles and San Diego, combined with its natural beauty and the extensive array of bouldering problems, ensures that Black Mountain remains a top bouldering spot in Southern California​​.
