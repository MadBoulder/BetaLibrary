    Bouldering in Maldà

    Maldà, located in the Lleida province of Catalonia, is one of the region's historic bouldering areas, nestled around the Sant Joan de Maldanell hermitage. This small yet significant sandstone area boasts unique problems with features like overhangs, slabs, and pockets. Climbers are drawn to the distinct character of each block, which ranges in orientation from north to south, making it accessible throughout the year. The wind often sweeps through this exposed location, offering relief in summer but adding an edge during colder months. The standout boulders, such as Dau and Submarí, present high-quality sandstone and classic problems that challenge climbers of all levels.

    The area’s charm lies in its serene rural setting combined with technical and physical routes. Some blocks require more advanced skills, with overhanging lines that test endurance and creativity. The landscape's peaceful beauty makes Maldà an ideal destination for those seeking a blend of sport and tranquility. Climbers should be prepared for varying weather conditions and ensure they respect the natural surroundings and local guidelines while enjoying this bouldering gem.
