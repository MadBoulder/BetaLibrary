    Bouldering in Sotunki

    Sotunki, located near Helsinki within the scenic Sipoonkorpi National Park, offers a peaceful and natural setting for bouldering. The area is characterized by granite boulders shaped by glacial activity, providing a mix of technical and powerful climbing. Problems range from accessible 4s and 5s to demanding 7s and 8s, with styles that include delicate slab climbing and overhanging compression moves. One of the most distinctive aspects of Sotunki is its remote yet accessible feel—just a short drive from the capital, yet immersed in deep forest surroundings.

    The best season to boulder in Sotunki is from late spring to early autumn, with the dry and cool conditions of early fall offering the best friction. However, summer days can still be enjoyable in the shaded forest environment. While Finland’s "Everyman’s Right" allows open access to nature, climbers should be mindful of conservation efforts in the national park. With its varied problems, quality rock, and tranquil setting, Sotunki is an excellent spot for those seeking both classic lines and new challenges in Southern Finland.
