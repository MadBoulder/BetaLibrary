    Bouldering in Rocklands

    Located in South Africa's Cederberg Mountains, Rocklands is a world-class bouldering destination celebrated for its stunning bright-orange sandstone boulders. This climber's paradise is known for its vast array of problems, with routes suitable for novices through to seasoned pros, featuring grades from beginner levels to challenging V15s. The variety of holds and styles, including dynamic moves on overhangs and delicate balancing on slabs, make Rocklands a comprehensive and versatile bouldering venue. Climbers often rave about the unique texture of the rock, which provides excellent grip, enhancing the climbing experience amidst the dramatic and rugged landscape.

    The optimal time to visit Rocklands is during the Southern Hemisphere’s winter, from June to September, when the cooler weather offers perfect conditions for climbing, minimizing the slickness of the rock and making for ideal friction. This period avoids the intense summer heat, making long climbing days more comfortable and productive. The area's remote beauty, combined with its legendary status in the climbing world, attracts adventurers from across the globe, making it not just a climbing trip but a pilgrimage to one of the most iconic bouldering spots on earth.
