    Bouldering in Labák

    While Labák is best known for its towering sandstone walls and traditional rope routes, it also offers a compelling bouldering experience. Here, climbers will find compact, high-friction sandstone blocks scattered through quiet pine forests, with problems featuring powerful overhangs. The best season for bouldering is from mid-April to late October, with autumn offering the driest and most reliable conditions for friction-dependent climbing.

    What truly sets Labák apart is its rich climbing heritage and the strict ethics tied to the sandstone. The bold style and scenic surroundings make climbing here feel like stepping into a different era. Whether you're here for the classic routes or the quiet blocs in the forest, Labák delivers a distinctly Czech climbing experience.
