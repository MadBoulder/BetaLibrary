    Bouldering in Cocalzinho de Goiás

    Set high in the wild Cerrado of the Serra dos Pireneus, Cocalzinho de Goiás is one of those places where climbing and nature blend into something special. Scattered across quartzite hills above 1,200 meters, you’ll find hundreds of problems on solid, fine-grained rock that mixes sharp crimps, pockets, and rounded slopers. The climbing style is diverse—steep compression lines, powerful roofs, and technical vertical faces—spread across iconic sectors like Casa da Cobra and Saci. Landings are usually friendly, approaches are short, and the whole area has that raw, open feel where you can move at your own pace and explore freely.

    The best time to climb here is during the dry season from May to September, when the rock stays clean, the friction is reliable, and access roads are manageable. While it’s possible to boulder year-round, the wet season can make things tricky. What really makes Cocalzinho stand out is its scale and energy—it’s a true hub of Brazilian bouldering, with festivals like Rockocal bringing the community together, yet on a normal day you can still find your own quiet corner among the blocs. Respect the land, tread lightly, and you’ll tap into a place that feels both powerful and grounded, where hard climbing and good vibes go hand in hand.
