    Bouldering in Caspe

    Caspe, in the province of Zaragoza, is an emerging bouldering destination set against the striking backdrop of the Ebro River and the Mequinenza reservoir. The sandstone boulders scattered throughout the area offer a mix of textures and holds, making for a varied climbing experience. The problems range from delicate technical climbs to powerful overhangs, catering to all levels.

    The best season for bouldering in Caspe is from November to March, when the Mediterranean climate provides crisp conditions and optimal friction. Some sectors remain climbable in October and April, but the warmer months can be too hot. The area's accessibility and proximity to Zaragoza, Barcelona, and Madrid make it a convenient getaway, while the nearby reservoir offers additional outdoor activities. Caspe remains a developing crag, with plenty of potential for new problems, making it an exciting and less crowded option for boulderers looking to explore something different in Spain.
