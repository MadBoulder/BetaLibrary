    Bouldering in Trowbarrow

    Trowbarrow Quarry is a striking bouldering destination in the Arnside & Silverdale AONB, known for its limestone formations and varied problems. The best time to visit is autumn and spring, when the friction is optimal, and conditions are dry. The most distinctive feature is the Shelter Stone, a massive freestanding block offering quality bouldering on steep overhangs and technical faces. The Red Wall area also provides classic challenges on sharp, well-featured limestone, catering to a range of skill levels.

    Despite being a quarry, the setting is scenic, with a mix of exposed rock and woodland, making for an enjoyable climbing experience. The limestone varies in quality, from compact and solid to more brittle sections requiring caution. The site is a designated nature reserve, so climbers should respect local guidelines, including restrictions on bolting and group use. Safety is a consideration, as sections of the Main Wall are monitored for stability. Overall, Trowbarrow provides a rewarding mix of technical and powerful bouldering in a peaceful yet adventurous environment.
