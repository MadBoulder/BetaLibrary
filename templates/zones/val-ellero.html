    Bouldering in Val Ellero

    Val Ellero, nestled in the scenic Piedmont region, is a bouldering gem known for its unique volcanic rock and technical climbing. The boulders here provide a distinctive experience, with intricate holds, slopers, and crimps demanding precise footwork and body positioning. The problems range from accessible warm-ups to world class test pieces, making it an excellent spot for both seasoned climbers and those looking to progress. The valley's natural beauty, combined with its historical significance in Italian bouldering, adds to the area's charm.

    The best time to visit is from spring to late autumn, as winter often brings snow, making access difficult. The approach is straightforward, with parking close to the main sectors, though the terrain can be rugged. While there are no campsites directly in the valley, nearby towns offer accommodation, and larger cities like Cuneo provide all necessary amenities.
