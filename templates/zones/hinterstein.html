    Bouldering in Hinterstein

    Hinterstein is a premier bouldering destination in the Allgäu region, known for its alpine beauty and diverse climbing styles. The area was shaped by a massive rockslide in the 1960s, resulting in a unique mix of dolomite and marl rock, which blends the characteristics of sandstone and gneiss. Climbers will find everything from delicate slabs to steep overhangs, making it a well-rounded playground for all skill levels. The circuit system offers well-organized progression from beginner-friendly problems to extreme challenges reaching up to 8b. With some problems reaching 10 meters, landings can be varied, so extra pads and spotters are often recommended.

    Best climbed in spring and autumn, Hinterstein dries quickly after rain but can be too sunny in midsummer and shadowed in winter. The boulders are spread across both an open blocky terrain and a more sheltered forested section, providing different conditions throughout the day. Standout problems like Powerload (8a) and Mjölnir (8a+/b) challenge even the strongest climbers, while circuits make navigation easier for those exploring the area. The breathtaking mountain views and varied climbing styles make Hinterstein a must-visit for boulderers looking for adventure in the northern Alps.
