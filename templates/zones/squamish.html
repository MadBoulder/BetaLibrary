    Bouldering in Squamish

    Squamish, located in British Columbia, Canada, is a world-renowned bouldering destination celebrated for its breathtaking granite boulders and stunning natural scenery. Nestled at the end of one of the Pacific Coast’s deepest fjords, Squamish offers an array of climbing features, from crimps to slopers, providing climbers with a diverse range of challenges. The area is famous for its high concentration of boulder problems, particularly in the Grand Wall Boulders area at the base of the Stawamus Chief, a massive granite monolith towering over 700 meters above the town. Climbers can find routes that cater to all skill levels, from beginner-friendly V0s to expert-level V14s​.

    The optimal season for bouldering in Squamish is typically from spring to fall, with the summer months offering the driest conditions and the best climbing experience under the canopy of old-growth forests. The mild, temperate climate ensures that climbing is possible year-round, though winter can bring rain rather than snow, keeping the boulders accessible. The vibrant climbing community, combined with the area's easy access and picturesque setting, makes Squamish a must-visit destination for bouldering enthusiasts from around the globe​​.
