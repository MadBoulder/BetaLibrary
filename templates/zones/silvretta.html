    Bouldering in Silvretta

    Tucked away in the Paznaun valley of Tyrol, Silvretta is a true alpine gem perched between 2,000 and 2,300 meters at the foot of Ballunspitze. The area boasts over 600 gneiss boulder problems with superb friction, ranging from easy circuits for kids to fierce testpieces. The rock is coarse and unforgiving, demanding solid skin management on longer stays. Climbers can expect technical, crimpy moves with many overhanging lines, all spread across a serene high-mountain landscape that offers escape from the valley heat. Thanks to well-maintained trails and efficient access via cable car or scenic hikes, Silvretta delivers a seamless blend of accessibility and adventure.

    The best time to boulder here is from June to October, when the cool alpine air provides ideal conditions—especially for those chasing friction-dependent sends. While the area attracts climbers from across Europe, its vastness ensures solitude is still possible, even during peak bouldering season. Families will appreciate the child-friendly circuits, and seasoned climbers will find plenty of challenges and potential for first ascents.
