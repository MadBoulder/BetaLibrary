Bouldering in India

In the realm of bouldering, India unfolds as an inviting canvas adorned with ancient landscapes and diverse rock formations.

A prominent highlight is Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site celebrated as the world's largest bouldering expanse. Within its historic ruins, climbers navigate granite challenges, with optimal conditions prevailing from November to February.

Manali, nestled in the northwest Himalayas, introduces gneiss and granite formations, contributing its unique allure to India's bouldering panorama. The climbing season spans April to June and September to November/December.

Turahalli, a mere 10 kilometers from Bangalore, stands as an isolated hill featuring excellent hard granite boulders, making it a noteworthy bouldering spot, particularly from November to March.

India's bouldering landscape is a tapestry of diverse terrains, each destination weaving a unique narrative through challenging climbs and breathtaking scenery.
